Kimchi

Description

This classic kimchi recipe using nappa cabbage and traditional korean ingredients is essential to your future korean cooking adventures. Created by Maangchi, this recipe makes a superb kimchi that's spicy, salty, sour, and most of all, delicious. Perfect for any korean meal, this side dish will add a flavorful kick to anything you eat with it.I hope you enjoy Maangchi's recipe just as much as I do!

Ingredients

  • 6 lbs nappa cabbage
  • 1/2 cup kosher salt
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tbsp sweet rice flour
  • 2 tbsp turbinado sugar
  • 2 cups radish matchsticks
  • 1 cup carrot matchsticks
  • 7-8 chopped green onions
  • 1 cup chopped buchu
  • 1 cup minari (optional)
  • 1/2 cup minced garlic cloves
  • 2 tsp minced ginger
  • 1 minced medium onion
  • 1/2 cup fish sauce
  • 1/4 cup chopped saeujeot (with brine)
  • 2 cups gochugaru

Steps

  1. Trim excess cabbage cores
  2. Halve each cabbage
  3. Cut 2 inch slit from center of each core
  4. Wet cabbages and salt each leaf generously
  5. Rest cabbages for 2 hours, turning every 30 minutes
  6. Wash cabbages to remove salt and dirt
  7. Quarter cabbage halves along slits, and cut off cores
  8. Put in strainer to drain
  9. Combine water and rice flour in a pot and cook for 10 minutes
  10. add sugar and cook 1 minute, then let cool off
  11. Pour mixture in bowl and combine with garlic, ginger, onion, fish sauce, saeujeot, and gochugaru
  12. Add in radish, carrot, green onion, buchu, and minari
  13. Spread paste on each cabbage leaf and place folded into a jar or plastic container
  14. Eat right away or ferment for a few days
